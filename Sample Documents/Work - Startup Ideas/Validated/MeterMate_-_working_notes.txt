MeterMate - working notes
=========================

Author: Avery Quinn   |   Doc #01   |   status: rough draft

The idea: MeterMate is a utility-bill analyzer that flags anomalies in household energy
use.


The problem
-----------
I keep coming back to the belief that pricing that stays free until a clear value moment.
When I talk to potential users, the frustration is never the headline feature - it's the
ten minutes of setup before anyone gets value. Note from a walk: kept thinking about
pricing that stays free until a clear value moment. People keep running into the same
wall: AI copilots for small, specific professional niches. Reminder to self: the tension
between open-source goodwill and paying the bills is the thing that actually matters here.
There's a real pull toward the tension between open-source goodwill and paying the bills,
and most existing tools ignore it.

Who it's for
------------
Reminder to self: AI copilots for small, specific professional niches is the thing that
actually matters here. If we tried to serve everyone we'd drown in scope. The danger of
building features nobody asked for is the wedge. A narrow user also makes the marketing
obvious - you know exactly which community to show up in. Note from a walk: kept thinking
about distribution through communities instead of paid ads. The first user is narrow on
purpose: a solo freelancer. Picking a tiny audience feels risky but it's the only way to
say no to the wrong features.

Why now
-------
Five years ago this would have needed a team. Now one person can ship the core in a
weekend. Reminder to self: the tension between open-source goodwill and paying the bills
is the thing that actually matters here. Two things changed: the tooling got cheap, and
people started caring about privacy as a feature rather than an afterthought. Note from a
walk: kept thinking about privacy as a feature rather than an afterthought. The risk is
building for a trend instead of a need. I want to check that distinction hard before
writing code.

How it could make money
-----------------------
Plan: free core with a paid team tier. If I forget everything else, don't forget that
distribution through communities instead of paid ads. Note from a walk: kept thinking
about pricing that stays free until a clear value moment. Honestly the revenue model is
the part I'm least sure about, and pretending otherwise would be a mistake. I keep
circling pricing that stays free until a clear value moment, because charging too early
scares off the exact people who would champion it.

----------------

months. Note from a walk: kept thinking about privacy as a feature rather than an
afterthought. Biggest risk: I never find the right community to launch in. If I forget
everything else, don't forget that the danger of building features nobody asked for.
There's also the open-source question - I want the goodwill, but goodwill doesn't pay
