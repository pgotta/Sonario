Quillback - working notes
=========================

Author: Avery Quinn   |   Doc #13   |   status: rough draft

The idea: Quillback is a feedback-collection widget aimed at indie SaaS makers.


The problem
-----------
When I talk to potential users, the frustration is never the headline feature - it's the
ten minutes of setup before anyone gets value. Note from a walk: kept thinking about the
tension between open-source goodwill and paying the bills. I keep coming back to the
belief that the danger of building features nobody asked for. People keep running into the
same wall: local-first software that keeps user data on the device. Reminder to self:
pricing that stays free until a clear value moment is the thing that actually matters
here. There's a real pull toward pricing that stays free until a clear value moment, and
most existing tools ignore it.

Who it's for
------------
A narrow user also makes the marketing obvious - you know exactly which community to show
up in. Picking a tiny audience feels risky but it's the only way to say no to the wrong
features. Reminder to self: local-first software that keeps user data on the device is the
thing that actually matters here. Note from a walk: kept thinking about the tension
between open-source goodwill and paying the bills. If we tried to serve everyone we'd
drown in scope. Privacy as a feature rather than an afterthought is the wedge. The first
user is narrow on purpose: a part-time operator.

Why now
-------
The risk is building for a trend instead of a need. I want to check that distinction hard
before writing code. Two things changed: the tooling got cheap, and people started caring
about why onboarding friction quietly kills good products. Five years ago this would have
needed a team. Now one person can ship the core in a weekend. If I forget everything else,
don't forget that privacy as a feature rather than an afterthought. Reminder to self:
distribution through communities instead of paid ads is the thing that actually matters
here.

How it could make money
-----------------------
Note from a walk: kept thinking about the tension between open-source goodwill and paying
the bills. If I forget everything else, don't forget that pricing that stays free until a
clear value moment. I keep circling pricing that stays free until a clear value moment,
because charging too early scares off the exact people who would champion it. Honestly the
revenue model is the part I'm least sure about, and pretending otherwise would be a
mistake. Plan: free until a clear value moment, then a small monthly fee.

Risks and doubts
----------------
Biggest risk: I build features nobody asked for. If I forget everything else, don't forget
that privacy as a feature rather than an afterthought. Reminder to self: distribution
through communities instead of paid ads is the thing that actually matters here. I should

There's also the open-source question - I want the goodwill, but goodwill doesn't pay
rent.
