TidePool - working notes
========================

Author: Avery Quinn   |   Doc #09   |   status: rough draft

The idea: TidePool is a local-first note system for field researchers with no signal.


The problem
-----------
There's a real pull toward AI copilots for small, specific professional niches, and most
existing tools ignore it. When I talk to potential users, the frustration is never the
headline feature - it's the ten minutes of setup before anyone gets value. Note from a
walk: kept thinking about shipping a rough version early to learn from real users. People
keep running into the same wall: privacy as a feature rather than an afterthought. I keep
coming back to the belief that why onboarding friction quietly kills good products.
Reminder to self: AI copilots for small, specific professional niches is the thing that
actually matters here.

Who it's for
------------
Picking a tiny audience feels risky but it's the only way to say no to the wrong features.
If we tried to serve everyone we'd drown in scope. The tension between open-source
goodwill and paying the bills is the wedge. A narrow user also makes the marketing obvious
- you know exactly which community to show up in. Reminder to self: local-first software
that keeps user data on the device is the thing that actually matters here. The first user
is narrow on purpose: a small nonprofit. Note from a walk: kept thinking about privacy as
a feature rather than an afterthought.

Why now
-------
Note from a walk: kept thinking about local-first software that keeps user data on the
device. The risk is building for a trend instead of a need. I want to check that
distinction hard before writing code. Reminder to self: AI copilots for small, specific
professional niches is the thing that actually matters here. Two things changed: the
tooling got cheap, and people started caring about the danger of building features nobody
asked for. Five years ago this would have needed a team. Now one person can ship the core
in a weekend.

How it could make money
-----------------------
Note from a walk: kept thinking about local-first software that keeps user data on the
device. I keep circling pricing that stays free until a clear value moment, because
charging too early scares off the exact people who would champion it. Reminder to self:
local-first software that keeps user data on the device is the thing that actually matters
here. Honestly the revenue model is the part I'm least sure about, and pretending
otherwise would be a mistake. Plan: pay-what-you-want with a suggested price.

Risks and doubts
----------------
If I forget everything else, don't forget that the tension between open-source goodwill
and paying the bills. Note from a walk: kept thinking about the tension between open-
source goodwill and paying the bills. Biggest risk: it solves a problem people have but
will not pay to fix. There's also the open-source question - I want the goodwill, but
goodwill doesn't pay rent. I should ship something rough and embarrassing early rather
than polish in the dark for months.
