Quillback - working notes
=========================

Author: Avery Quinn   |   Doc #12   |   status: rough draft

The idea: Quillback is a feedback-collection widget aimed at indie SaaS makers.


The problem
-----------
People keep running into the same wall: local-first software that keeps user data on the
device. Note from a walk: kept thinking about distribution through communities instead of
paid ads. When I talk to potential users, the frustration is never the headline feature -
it's the ten minutes of setup before anyone gets value. I keep coming back to the belief
that tools that reduce busywork for solo operators and freelancers. There's a real pull
toward local-first software that keeps user data on the device, and most existing tools
ignore it. If I forget everything else, don't forget that distribution through communities
instead of paid ads.

Who it's for
------------
Note from a walk: kept thinking about the danger of building features nobody asked for. If
we tried to serve everyone we'd drown in scope. Ai copilots for small, specific
professional niches is the wedge. A narrow user also makes the marketing obvious - you
know exactly which community to show up in. The first user is narrow on purpose: a part-
time operator. If I forget everything else, don't forget that AI copilots for small,
specific professional niches. Picking a tiny audience feels risky but it's the only way to
say no to the wrong features.

Why now
-------
Five years ago this would have needed a team. Now one person can ship the core in a
weekend. Two things changed: the tooling got cheap, and people started caring about tools
that reduce busywork for solo operators and freelancers. Note from a walk: kept thinking
about AI copilots for small, specific professional niches. Reminder to self: AI copilots
for small, specific professional niches is the thing that actually matters here. The risk
is building for a trend instead of a need. I want to check that distinction hard before
writing code.

How it could make money
-----------------------
Plan: free until a clear value moment, then a small monthly fee. If I forget everything
else, don't forget that local-first software that keeps user data on the device. Honestly
the revenue model is the part I'm least sure about, and pretending otherwise would be a

kept thinking about privacy as a feature rather than an afterthought.
